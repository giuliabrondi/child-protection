Our Mission

We are dedicated to the prevention of child abuse and exploitation through education, awareness, and direct support for children and families.

Every child deserves to grow up in a safe, nurturing environment. Our initiative works tirelessly to create a world where childhood is protected and cherished.

  • Advocate for stronger policies and laws to protect children
  • Support research into effective prevention and intervention strategies

Recognizing Warning Signs

Being able to identify potential signs of abuse or exploitation is the first step in protecting children. Learn to recognize these important warning signs.

Physical Abuse

Physical abuse involves non-accidental physical injury to a child.

  • Unexplained burns, bruises, or welts in patterns
  • Fear of adults or certain individuals
  • Flinching when touched or approached
  • Wearing inappropriate clothing to cover injuries
  • Injuries in various stages of healing

Emotional Abuse

Emotional abuse involves behaviors that harm a child's emotional development or sense of self-worth.

  • Extreme behavior changes or developmental delays
  • Lack of attachment to parents or caregivers
  • Depression, anxiety, or withdrawal
  • Excessive perfectionism or fear of making mistakes
  • Attempts to take on adult roles or responsibilities

Sexual Abuse

Sexual abuse includes any sexual activity with a child, including touching and non-touching offenses.

  • Difficulty walking or sitting
  • Knowledge of sexual acts inappropriate for age
  • Reluctance to change clothes or participate in activities
  • Sudden changes in behavior or school performance
  • Running away from home

Neglect

Neglect occurs when a parent or caregiver fails to provide adequate supervision, food, clothing, medical care, or education.

  • Consistent hunger, poor hygiene, or inappropriate dress
  • Unattended medical or dental needs
  • Consistent lack of supervision
  • Frequent absences from school
  • Being left alone for extended periods

Online Exploitation

Online exploitation involves using the internet to manipulate or abuse children.

  • Secretive online behavior
  • Receiving gifts or money from unknown sources
  • Spending increased time online
  • Having phone calls from unknown adults
  • Withdrawal from family and friends

Trafficking

Child trafficking involves the recruitment, transportation, or harboring of children for exploitation.

  • Chronic running away
  • Presence of older "boyfriend" or "girlfriend"
  • Multiple phones or hotel key cards
  • Unexplained access to money or luxury items
  • Signs of physical abuse or malnourishment

Prevention Strategies

Prevention is the most effective way to protect children. Learn about strategies that can help create safer environments for all children.

For Parents & Caregivers

Parents and caregivers play a crucial role in creating safe environments for children.

  • Talk to children about personal boundaries and appropriate touch
  • Create open communication where children feel safe to discuss concerns
  • Monitor online activities and teach internet safety
  • Know your children's friends and their families
  • Learn to recognize warning signs of abuse

For Communities

Communities can create networks of protection for children.

  • Support child-focused education and prevention programs
  • Encourage background checks for those working with children
  • Create safe reporting mechanisms for concerns
  • Support families under stress with resources and assistance
  • Advocate for child protection policies in organizations

For Professionals

Professionals working with children have unique responsibilities in prevention.

  • Complete comprehensive training on recognizing abuse
  • Understand mandatory reporting requirements
  • Implement trauma-informed approaches
  • Create policies that minimize one-on-one adult-child interactions
  • Document and report concerns promptly

Getting Help

If you suspect a child is being abused or is at risk, it's important to take action. Here's how you can get help.

If a child is in immediate danger

Call emergency services (911 in the US) immediately if you believe a child is in immediate danger.

Reporting Child Abuse or Neglect

Report suspected child abuse or neglect to your local child protective services agency or law enforcement. Most areas have 24-hour hotlines for reporting.

National Child Abuse Hotline (US): 1-800-4-A-CHILD (1-800-422-4453)

For Survivors Seeking Support

If you are a survivor of child abuse and need support, resources are available to help you heal.

RAINN (Rape, Abuse & Incest National Network): 1-800-656-HOPE (4673)

For Those With Harmful Thoughts

If you are having thoughts about harming a child, reach out for help immediately.

Stop It Now! Helpline: 1-888-PREVENT (1-888-773-8368)
